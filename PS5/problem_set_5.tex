\problem{}
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Let $f\colon X\to Y$ be a map of sets, and let $y\in Y$ be a point. Show that the fibre product $y\times_Y X$ can be identified with the fibre $f^{-1}(y)$.
 \item Let $f_i\colon X_i\to S,\ i=1,2,$ be morphisms of schemes, and let $x\in X_1$ be a point with $k(x)=k(f_1(x))$. Show that the fibre of $X_1\times_S X_2$ over $x$ is isomorphic to the fibre of $f_2$ over $f_1(x)$.
\end{enumerate}

\problem{}
Let $F$ be a presheaf (of sets) on $X$, and $x\in X$ a point. Show that the stalk of the sheafification $F^{sh}$ at $x$ is isomorphic to $F_x$.

\problem{}
Let $a\colon F\to G$ be a morphism of sheaves (of sets) on $X$. Show that $a$ is an epimorphism if and only if $a_x\colon F_x\to G_x$ is surjective for every $x\in X$.

\problem{}
Let $s\in F(U)$ be a section of the sheaf of Abelian groups $F$ over the open subset $U\subseteq X$. Show that the support of $s$, i.e. the set $\{x\in U|0\neq s_x\in F_x\}$, is closed in $U$.

\problem{}
Construct a scheme whose underlying topological space consists of three points $X=\{p,q,\eta\}$, such that $p$ and $q$ are closed, and $\eta$ is open.
