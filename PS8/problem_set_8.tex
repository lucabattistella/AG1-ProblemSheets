\problem[(5 pts)]{}
Automorphisms of $\PP^n(k)$ induced by linear automorphisms of $k^{n+1}$ are called \emph{projectivities}.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Show that every automorphism of $\PP^1(k)$ is a \emph{projectivity}, which can be written as \[z\mapsto\frac{az+b}{cz+d}.\]
  \item Compute the stabiliser $\on{Stab}_G([1:0])$ in $G=PGL_2(k)$ of the point $[1:0]\in\PP^1(k)$, and $\on{Stab}_G([1:0])\cap\on{Stab}_G([0:1])$.
 \item Show that the action of $PGL_2(k)$ on $\PP^1(k)$ is sharply $3$-transitive (for every two triples of distinct points there is exactly one projectivity moving one to the other).
 \item The \emph{cross-ratio} of four distinct points $(z_1,z_2,z_3,z_4)$ of $\PP^1(k)\setminus\{\infty\}$ is defined as:
 \[\frac{(z_1-z_3)(z_2-z_4)}{(z_1-z_4)(z_2-z_3)}\in k.\]
 Extend this definition in case one of the points is $\infty$. Show that projectivities preserve the cross-ratio. Conclude that the action of $PGL_2(k)$ on $\PP_1(k)$ is not $4$-transitive in general. (Bonus: what are the possible values of the cross-ratio? What happens in the limit when two points come together (over $\CC$)?)
 
\end{enumerate}

\problem[(4 pts)]{}
More on projectivities.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Show that the action of $PGL_{n+1}(k)$ on $\PP^n(k)$ is $2$-transitive, but not $3$-transitive in general.
 \item An $(n+2)$-tuple of points in $\PP^n$ is said to be \emph{in general (linear) position} if no $n+1$ of them are contained in a hyperplane. Show that $PGL_{n+1}(k)$ acts transitively on the set of $(n+2)$-tuples in general position.
 \item Let $U=D_p(f)\subset\PP^n(k)$ be the complement of a hypersurface of degree $d$. Identify $U$ with an affine algebraic variety.
 \item Show that $PGL_{n+1}(k)$ is an affine algebraic variety.
\end{enumerate}

\problem[(5 pts)]{}
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Let $X$ be an irreducible projective variety of dimension $n$. Show that the affine cone $C_X$ is irreducible of dimension $n+1$.
 \item Let $X$ and $Y$ be projective subvarieties of $\PP^n(k)$ such that $\dim(X)+\dim(Y)\geq n$. Then the intersection of $X$ and $Y$ is not empty.
 \item Show that $\PP^m\times\PP^n$ is not isomorphic to $\PP^{m+n}$ (unless $m$ or $n$ is $0$).
 \item Show that there is no morphism $\PP^N\to\PP^n$ for $N>n$ (unless $n=0$).
\end{enumerate}

\problem[(2 pts)]{}
Show that the diagonal of a scheme is always a locally closed immersion (closed in an open).
