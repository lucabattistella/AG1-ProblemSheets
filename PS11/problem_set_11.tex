\problem{}
Consider the conic $C=V_+(x_0x_1-x_2^2)\subseteq\PP^2$ and the lines $L_1=V_+(x_1),\ L_2=V_+(x_0-x_1)$. Compute the intersection of $C$ with $L_i,\ i=1,2$ (with multiplicities).

\problem{}
An irreducible curve of degree $d$ in $\PP^n$ is contained in a linear subspace of dimension at most $d$.

\problem{}
A dimension $0$ Noetherian local ring is regular if and only if it is a field.

A dimension $1$ Noetherian local ring is regular if and only if it is a PID. (You can take for granted this version of the Artin-Rees lemma: if $(A,\mathfrak{m})$ is a local Noetherian ring, then $\bigcap_{i=0}^\infty\mathfrak{m}^i=0$.)

\problem{}
\emph{The corank of the Jacobian matrix does not depend on the presentation.}

Let $R=k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]/(f_1,\ldots,f_r)$ and $\underline{a}\in k^n$ such that $f_i(\underline{a})=0$ for all $i=1,\ldots,r$.

Show that the corank of $\on{Jac}(F)(\underline{a})$ is unaffected by:
\begin{enumerate}
 \item adding to $(f_1,\ldots,f_r)$ another polynomial from the ideal they generate;
 \item adding to the generators of $R$ a new variable $x_{n+1}$, and to the ideal a new relation of the form $x_{n+1}-g(x_1,\ldots,x_n)$. 
\end{enumerate}


\problem{}\emph{Projective Jacobian criterion.}
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Let $f\in k[x_0,\ldots,x_d]$ be a homogeneous polynomial of degree $d$. Then $\sum_{i=0}^nx_i\frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i}=d\cdot f$.
 \item Let $\underline{a}\in X\subseteq \PP^n(k)$, with $I(X)=\langle f_1,\ldots,f_r\rangle$. Prove that $X$ is smooth at $\underline{a}$ if and only if the rank of the $r\times(n+1)$ matrix $\on{Jac}(F)(\underline{a})=\left(\frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}(\underline{a})\right)$ is at least $n-\on{dim}_aX$ (note that the matrix is only well-defined up to scalar, which does not affect the rank).
 \item Let $X\subseteq\PP^3(k)$ be the twisted cubic $V_+(x_0x_2-x_1^3,x_0x_3-x_1x_2,x_1x_3-x_2^2)$. Use the previous point to show that $X$ is smooth.
\end{enumerate}
