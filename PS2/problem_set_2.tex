\textit{Every problem is worth 2 points.}

\problem{}
Let $X$ be a Hausdorff topological space (i.e. for every pair of distinct points $x,y\in X$ there exist disjoint open subsets $U,V\subseteq X$ such that $x\in U,y\in V$). A subspace of $X$ is irreducible if and only if it is a singleton.

\problem{}
Let $f\colon X\to Y$ be a continuous and open map of topological spaces. Suppose that $Y$ is irreducible, and so is every fibre of $f$. Then $X$ is irreducible as well.

\problem{}
Let $X$ be an irreducible topological space of finite dimension $n$. For any subspace $Y\subseteq X$, $\dim(Y)\leq\dim(X)$.

\problem{}
Let $\varphi\colon R\to S$ be a homomorphism of local rings. Show that $\varphi^{-1}(\mathfrak{m}_S)=\mathfrak{m}_R$ if and only if $\varphi(\mathfrak{m}_R)\subseteq\mathfrak{m}_S$.

\problem{}
Let $GL_n(k)$ denote the set of invertible $n\times n$ matrices over $k$. Show that it is an affine algebraic variety of dimension $n^2$.
