\problem{}
Show that every affine algebraic set over $\mathbb{R}$ is the vanishing locus of a single polynomial.

\problem{}
Let $k$ be a field. Show that there exist infinitely many irreducible polynomials in $k[x]$.

\problem{}
Let $k$ be an algebraically closed field. Show that the following formulations of Hilbert's Nullstellensatz are equivalent.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item If $I$ is a proper ideal of $k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$, then $V(I)\neq\emptyset$.
 \item Every maximal ideal of $k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$ is of the form \[\mathfrak{m}_{\underline{a}}=(x_1-a_1,\ldots,x_n-a_n).\]
\end{enumerate}


\problem{}
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Let $f\colon X\to Y$ be a continuous map of topological spaces. Show that $f(X)$ is connected (resp. irreducible) if $X$ is.
 \item Let $X$ be an affine algebraic set over $\mathbb{C}$. Show that $\operatorname{id}\colon X_{Eucl}\to X_{Zar}$ is continuous.
 \item Show that $\mathbb{A}^1(\mathbb{C})$ is connected.
\end{enumerate}

\problem{}
Show that the vanishing locus of $(x_2-x_1^2,x_3-x_1^3)$ is irreducible. Can you find a parametric presentation of it? This is called a twisted cubic.
