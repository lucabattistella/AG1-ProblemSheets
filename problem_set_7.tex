\problem{}
Let $Y$ be an algebraic prevariety, and let $X\subseteq Y$ be a closed subset. Show that $X$ admits a unique structure of prevariety making $\iota\colon X\hookrightarrow Y$ into a closed embedding (a morphism of locally ringed spaces such that $\OO_Y\twoheadrightarrow\iota_*\OO_X$).

\problem{}
Automorphisms of $\PP^n(k)$ induced by linear automorphisms of $k^{n+1}$ are called \emph{projectivities}.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Show that every automorphism of $\PP^1(k)$ is a \emph{projectivity}, and it can be written as \[z\mapsto\frac{az+b}{cz+d}.\]
 \item Show that the action of $PGL_2(k)$ on $\PP^1(k)$ is sharply $3$-transitive (for every two triples of distinct points there is exactly one projectivity moving one to the other).
 \item The \emph{cross-ratio} of four distinct points $(z_1,z_2,z_3,z_4)$ of $\PP^1(k)\setminus\{\infty\}$ is defined as:
 \[\frac{(z_1-z_3)(z_2-z_4)}{(z_1-z_4)(z_2-z_3)}.\]
 Extend this definition in case one of the points is $\infty$. Show that projectivities preserve the cross-ratio. Conclude that the action of $PGL_2(k)$ on $\PP_1(k)$ is not $4$-transitive in general. (Bonus: what is the codomain of this map? What happens in the limit when two points come together (over $\CC$)?)
 \item Show that the action of $PGL_{n+1}(k)$ on $\PP^n(k)$ is $2$-transitive, but not $3$-transitive in general.
 \item An $(n+2)$-tuple of points in $\PP^n$ is said to be \emph{in general (linear) position} if no $n+1$ of them are contained in a hyperplane. Show that $PGL_{n+1}(k)$ acts transitively on the set of $(n+2)$-tuples in general position.
\end{enumerate}

\problem{}
Let $k$ be an algebraically closed field of characteristic different from $2$.
\begin{enumerate}
 \item Let $X_2\subseteq\Aaff^2(k)$ be the vanishing locus of an irreducible polynomial of degree $2$. Show that after an affine (linear $+$ translation) change of coordinates, $X_2$ can be identified with the vanishing locus of either $f_1=x_1^2-x_2$ or $f_2=x_1x_2-1$.
 \item Classify all conics (hypersurfaces of degree $2$) in $\PP^2(k)$.
 \item Show that an integral conic is isomorphic to $\PP^1(k)$.
 \item Bonus: find a field $k$ and an integral conic in $\PP^2(k)$ that is not isomorphic to $\PP^1(k)$.
\end{enumerate}
